Why is it Important to Donate Bl

About 328 million people currently live in the U.S. Each year, approximately 6.8 million people in the U.S. donate blood. Annually, this adds up to about 13.6 million units of whole blood collected for donation in the U.S. The Red Cross provides about 40% of the nation’s blood and blood cell components to donors. Your blood donations are used for patients in need of surgery, cancer treatment, and transfusions for blood loss from traumatic injuries.
Want to know more? Visit the Facts About Blood Supply page.

Why Cancer Patients Need Blood ?

For cancer patients, blood transfusions help replenish platelets lost during chemo or radiation therapy. Blood cell production, which occurs in the bone marrow, is often at risk. This can lead to chronic diseases, affecting organs like the kidneys, spleen, and liver.

Why CMV Negative Blood is Important?

CMV (Cytomegalovirus) is a flu-like virus that affects 85% of adults by age 40. CMV-negative blood is used for newborns, premature babies, and immune-compromised adults due to its reduced risk of infection.

Whole Blood Donation vs
Power Red Donation

Whole blood donation includes all blood components: red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma. It is used to help patients with cancer, blood disorders, and trauma injuries.

Power Red donation allows donors to give two units of red blood cells in one appointment. This method is more efficient, returning the other blood components to the donor.

Platelet Donation vs. Plasma Donation

Platelet Donation

Platelets help with blood clotting. During platelet donation, blood is drawn from one arm, separated, and returned through the other arm. Platelets are vital for cancer and trauma patients.

Plasma Donation

Plasma donation, or plasmapheresis, extracts plasma from the blood and returns the other components. Plasma helps treat severe burns, cancer, and life-threatening conditions.

The Red Cross encourages donors with AB blood type to donate plasma, as they are universal plasma donors. One donation provides up to four units of plasma for patients in need.

As you can see, blood donation is vital. There is no substitute for blood, and it cannot be manufactured. Every day, blood donors help patients of all ages and backgrounds.
